GreenGuardians

The Complete Guide to Plant Health Analysis & Treatment

This comprehensive guide explains every step of using (or building) GreenGuardians — an AI-powered agricultural assistant that helps farmers diagnose plant diseases, analyze soil conditions, and receive treatment recommendations.

Why This Project Matters

  • Saves Crops: Early detection can prevent 40-60% yield loss
  • Reduces Costs: Avoid unnecessary pesticide use
  • Empowers Farmers: No need for expensive lab tests

1 Take a Photo

Why This Step Matters

Farmers often struggle to identify early signs of disease or nutrient deficiencies. A simple photo can reveal critical insights that the naked eye might miss.

How to Do It Right

  1. Use Your Smartphone: Any modern phone camera works (5MP or higher recommended)
  2. Lighting Matters:
    • Best: Natural daylight (avoid harsh shadows)
    • Avoid: Flash (can distort colors)
  3. Focus on Problem Areas:
    • For leaf diseases: Capture both sides of affected leaves
    • For soil analysis: Take a close-up of soil texture and color
  4. Upload Existing Photos: Already have images? Upload from your gallery
Technical Insight (For Developers)
  • Image Processing: The system uses edge detection to enhance blurry images
  • Minimum Resolution: 1024×768 pixels for accurate AI analysis

2 Let AI Analyze

What Happens Behind the Scenes

  1. Disease Detection:
    • Compares your photo against 50,000+ plant disease images (trained on datasets like PlantVillage)
    • Identifies common issues: fungal infections, pest damage, viral spots
  2. Soil & Nutrient Analysis:
    • Estimates pH levels from soil color and texture
    • Detects nutrient deficiencies (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium)
  3. Real-Time Processing:
    • Results in under 10 seconds (even offline with cached models)

Farmer-Friendly Results

Simple Alerts: "Your maize shows early signs of Turcicum Leaf Blight (70% confidence)."

Risk Levels:

✅ Low risk (monitor)

⚠️ Moderate (take action)

❌ Critical (urgent treatment needed)

For Developers: How the AI Works
  • Model: Custom CNN (Convolutional Neural Network) trained on Ethiopian crop varieties
  • Open-Source Alternative: TensorFlow Lite for mobile deployment

3 View the Results

Key Features of the Report

  1. Visual Heatmaps:
    • Red zones = Severe damage
    • Yellow zones = Early warning
  2. Nutrient Breakdown:
    • "Your soil has low Nitrogen (2.1%) — ideal range: 4-6%."
  3. Historical Tracking:
    • Compare with past scans to monitor progress

How Farmers Use This Data

  • Prioritize Actions: Treat the most affected plants first
  • Share with Experts: Export PDF reports to agronomists
Technical Deep Dive
  • Data Formats: JSON API for integration with farm management software
  • Offline Mode: Stores last 50 scans locally

4 Get Treatment Advice

Tailored Recommendations

Organic Solutions (Preferred)

Example: "Spray neem oil every 7 days for 3 weeks."

  • Safe for beneficial insects
  • Low environmental impact
Chemical Treatments (Severe Cases)

Example: "Apply Mancozeb 80% WP at 2g/liter water."

  • Fast-acting for critical infections
  • Precise dosage instructions
Preventive Measures
  • Crop rotation schedules
  • Soil amendment tips (e.g., compost for low pH)

Cost & Accessibility Filters

  • Low-Cost Options: Highlights DIY remedies (e.g., garlic/chili sprays)
  • Local Availability: Recommends products available in Ethiopian markets
Building the Recommendation Engine
  • Knowledge Base: Curated from FAO and Ethiopian Agricultural Research Institute
  • Dynamic Pricing: Pulls real-time data from local agro-input dealers

For Developers: Project Roadmap

  • Phase 2: Expand to 20 crops (2024)
  • Phase 3: Soil sensor integration (2025)
