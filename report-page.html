Disease Identified:
Early Blight (Alternaria solani)

Early blight is a common fungal disease that affects tomatoes, potatoes, and other plants in the nightshade family. It causes dark concentric rings on leaves that resemble targets, often starting with older leaves. The disease thrives in warm, humid conditions and can significantly reduce yield if left untreated.

Recommended Treatments

Organic Treatment
  • Apply neem oil spray every 7-10 days
  • Use baking soda spray (1 tbsp baking soda + 1 tsp vegetable oil + 1/2 tsp soap in 1 gallon water)
  • Apply compost tea to strengthen plant immunity
  • Remove and destroy infected plant material
Environmental Management
  • Improve air circulation by proper plant spacing
  • Water at the base of plants (avoid wetting leaves)
  • Use mulch to prevent soil splashing onto leaves
  • Rotate crops annually (3-year rotation recommended)
  • Remove weeds that may harbor the fungus
Chemical Treatment
  • Apply copper-based fungicides (follow label instructions)
  • Use chlorothalonil-based products for severe infections
  • Apply fungicides preventatively before symptoms appear
  • Rotate between different fungicide classes to prevent resistance
Always follow manufacturer instructions when using chemical treatments
